# Sephar Studios — STC Token: Plain English Guide

> Written for anyone, no crypto knowledge needed.
> Think of STC (Studio Token) like loyalty points you earn on Netflix — except these points have real value, can be traded, and the more people use the platform, the more valuable they become.

## What Is STC and Why Does It Exist?

Imagine if Netflix gave you points every time you watched a movie. Now imagine those points could:
- Pay for your next month's subscription
- Give you discounts on your bill
- Be bought and sold like shares
- Go up in value the more popular Netflix gets

That is exactly what STC does for Sephar Studios. It is the platform's own loyalty currency that lives on the blockchain (Polygon network) — meaning nobody can fake it, nobody can take it from you, and every transaction is publicly verifiable.

**Important:** You earn a maximum of 5 STC per day no matter how much you watch. This is a safety cap so the reward pool doesn't run out too quickly.

## What Can You Do With STC?

Think of STC as having three uses — like a multi-purpose gift card:

### Use 1: Pay for a Free Month
Spend 200 STC (at the right time — see the phase plan below) and get one free month of Basic access. Your 200 STC goes back to the platform treasury to be reused, not destroyed.

**Cooldown rule:** After using it the first time, you must wait 100 days before using it again. After the second time, wait 200 days. Then it alternates: 100 days, 200 days, 100 days... This stops people from abusing the system.

### Use 2: Lock It Up for a Discount (Staking)
"Staking" just means locking your STC in a digital safe for a set period. In return, you get a permanent discount on your subscription while it's locked:

| STC Locked | How Long | Your Discount |
|---|---|---|
| 1,000 STC | 3 months – 1 year | 10% off every bill |
| 1,000 STC | 2 years | 20% off every bill |
| 3,500 STC | 3 months – 1 year | 20% off every bill |
| 3,500 STC | 2 years | 35% off every bill |
| 10,000 STC | 3 months – 1 year | 35% off every bill |
| 10,000 STC | 2 years | 50% off every bill |
| 35,000 STC | Any time | 50% off every bill |

**Real-world example:**
> James locks 10,000 STC for 2 years. His $10/month Basic plan now costs him $5/month. Over 2 years he saves $120. If he earned those 10,000 STC by watching (which takes about 2,000 days naturally), or he bought them — the savings keep coming every single month while the STC is locked.

### Use 3: Buy or Sell on the Exchange
The platform has its own internal exchange (called an AMM — think of it like a currency exchange booth at an airport). You can:
- **Buy STC** by paying with USDC (a digital dollar)
- **Sell STC** back for USDC if you want to cash out
- The price is set by supply and demand — the more people buy, the higher the price goes

## Where Does the Money Go When You Subscribe?

Every time someone pays their subscription fee, it is automatically split:

```
You pay $10/month

$5.50  → Platform keeps this (pays for servers, staff, development)
$3.00  → Goes to a pool that pays creators
$0.80  → Used to BUY STC tokens and DESTROY them (making remaining STC more valuable)
$0.40  → Goes into the pool that pays users' STC rewards
$0.30  → Emergency reserve fund
```

**The $0.80 buyback is the most important piece.** Every subscription, the platform spends 80 cents buying STC from the exchange and burning it (permanently destroying it). This means:
- With 100,000 subscribers → $80,000/month is spent buying and burning STC
- Fewer tokens in existence + same demand = each token is worth more
- This is why early STC holders benefit the most from platform growth

## How the Token Price Grows

The price of STC goes up for three reasons:

**Reason 1: Burning reduces supply**
Every time STC is burned (from buybacks), there are fewer tokens in the world. Like a limited-edition sneaker — the fewer that exist, the more each one is worth.

**Reason 2: Staking locks supply**
When people stake STC for discounts, those tokens are temporarily removed from circulation. If 10% of all tokens are locked, there are 10% fewer tokens available to buy — pushing the price up.

**Reason 3: The revenue multiplier**
The platform's exchange has a built-in "growth bonus" — as the platform earns more money, the displayed STC price gets a multiplier applied to it:

| Platform Monthly Revenue | Price Bonus |
|---|---|
| $0 | No bonus (1×) |
| $10,000/month (~1,000 subscribers) | 1.5× bonus |
| $20,000/month (~2,000 subscribers) | 2× bonus |
| $40,000/month+ (~4,000+ subscribers) | 3× bonus (maximum) |

This means even at just 4,000 subscribers the AMM shows STC at triple its raw trading price. Early holders see their portfolio value multiply just from the platform growing.

## The 2B vs 5B Question — In Simple Terms

Should the total supply be increased from 2 billion to 5 billion?

**Simple answer: No. Here is why:**

Imagine you own 1% of a pizza. The pizza has 8 slices.
- **Option A (keep 2B):** The pizza stays 8 slices. Your 1 slice is worth more as the pizza becomes more desirable.
- **Option B (go 5B):** The pizza is now 20 slices. You still own 1% — but 2.5 slices. Each slice is thinner. The pizza hasn't gotten bigger, just cut smaller.

Increasing supply does not make the platform more successful. It just makes each token worth less per dollar of buyback. The right way to extend the token's life is through **smart design** — specifically making creators lock up tokens instead of just receiving and selling them.

## The 200 STC Subscription — The Tricky Part

This is the most nuanced part of the whole system. Here is the honest breakdown:

**The problem:**
200 STC was chosen as "1 month of subscription." But 200 STC is not always worth $10. Its worth depends on the STC price:

| If STC is worth... | 200 STC costs the user... | Is this fair to the platform? |
|---|---|---|
| $0.001 (very cheap) | $0.20 | ❌ No — platform gets 20 cents for a $10 subscription |
| $0.01 (early price) | $2.00 | ⚠️ Generous but manageable with the cooldown |
| $0.05 (mature price) | $10.00 | ✅ Yes — perfect balance |
| $0.10 (high price) | $20.00 | ❌ Too expensive — users just pay fiat instead |

**The sweet spot is $0.05/STC.** Below that, users get too good a deal. Above that, no one uses the feature.

**The cooldown protection:**
Because of the 100/200 day cooldown rule, each user can only do this about 2.4 times per year maximum. So even if STC is cheap, one user can only get 2.4 free months per year this way — limiting the damage.

**The recommended approach (no code changes needed):**
| Phase | STC Price | Subscription Cost | What the user pays | Platform receives |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Launch phase | Below $0.02 | **500 STC** | ~$5 | ~$5 — still a discount, not exploitable |
| Growth phase | $0.02–$0.05 | **300 STC** | ~$6–$15 | Users feel rewarded as cost drops |
| Mature phase | Above $0.05 | **200 STC** | ~$10+ | Full economic balance |

The owner can change this number at any time with one transaction — no rebuilding required.

## Why Creators Are Key to Everything

Creators are the people making the films and shows. Here is their current deal:

- They receive 30% of all revenue their content generates, paid in USDC (digital dollars)
- They can also receive STC bonuses for hitting milestones (first 1,000 views, trending, monthly top creator etc.)

**The important upgrade to add:**
Right now creators only *receive* STC — they don't have a reason to hold it. This means they often sell it, which pushes the price down.

A better design gives creators a reason to *lock up* STC:

| Creator Level | Lock Up This Much STC | Earn This Revenue Share |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (free) | 0 STC | 30% |
| Verified | 5,000 STC locked | 40% |
| Pro Creator | 15,000 STC locked | 50% |
| Studio Creator | 50,000 STC locked | 55% |

With 1,000 professional creators each locking 15,000 STC: **15 million tokens are locked up**, taken out of circulation, which pushes the price up for everyone — including the founders.

The 1 billion STC set aside for user rewards — how quickly will it run out?

| Active Daily Users | Without any buyback | With 8% buyback from subscriptions |
|---|---|---|

As the founders, there are four ways the business makes money:

**1. Direct platform revenue**
