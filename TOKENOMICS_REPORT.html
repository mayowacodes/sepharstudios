TokenAMM.sol applies a dynamic multiplier to the reported STC price based on platform revenue. This is a built-in appreciation mechanism that rewards early holders as the platform grows.

This is achievable at roughly 4,000 Basic subscribers. Early liquidity providers benefit from this multiplier immediately upon threshold crossing.

Pool Sustainability Analysis

The platform has two complementary sustainability mechanisms: the initial 1B STC pool and the ongoing revenue buyback. Sustainability is achieved when monthly buyback ≥ monthly emissions.

Monthly emission = DAU × dailyUserRewardLimit × 30 Monthly buyback (STC) = (subscribers × avg_price × 0.08) ÷ STC_market_price Self-sustain condition: Monthly buyback ≥ Monthly emission At STC = $0.01/token, DAU = 100,000: Emission = 100,000 × 5 × 30 = 15,000,000 STC/month Buyback needed = 15,000,000 × $0.01 = $150,000 USDC/month Subscribers needed = $150,000 ÷ (12 × 0.08) = 156,250 paying subs

Section 08

The 200 STC Subscription — Full Analysis

The mintSubscriptionWithSTC() function allows users to spend 200 STC (the stcSubscriptionAmount variable, adjustable by owner) to receive one month of Basic access. This section examines every aspect of this mechanism.

8.1 What the Contract Actually Does

  • User must first call stcToken.approve(subscriptionContract, 200e18)
  • 200 STC is transferred from the user's wallet to platformTreasury (not burned)
  • A Basic-tier subscription NFT is minted or the user's existing NFT is extended by 30 days
  • The redemption count is incremented; cooldown is applied based on whether count is odd or even
  • The amountPaid field in the Subscription struct is set to 0 (STC payment, not USDC)
  • No revenue distribution occurs — the distributeRevenue() function is NOT called
⚠ Critical Gap: No Revenue Distribution on STC Redemptions When a user redeems 200 STC for a subscription, the creators receive $0 in USDC from that transaction. The 30% creator share, 8% buyback, and 4% user rewards that apply to fiat subscriptions do not occur. Only the platform treasury receives the 200 STC. This is economically sound for platform sustainability but creates a hidden discrepancy: creators earn nothing from STC-redeemed subscriptions. This should be documented in creator agreements.

8.3 The Cooldown Mechanism — Does It Protect?

The alternating cooldown (100 days → 200 days → 100 days → 200 days) limits a single user to approximately 2.4 redemptions per year on average.

Cooldown cycle: Redemption 1 → wait 100 days Redemption 2 → wait 200 days Redemption 3 → wait 100 days Redemption 4 → wait 200 days Average cooldown = (100 + 200) ÷ 2 = 150 days per redemption Redemptions per year = 365 ÷ 150 = ~2.4 per user Max annual STC spent per user on subscriptions = 2.4 × 200 = 480 STC/year Max annual fiat savings per user = 2.4 × $10 = $24/year

At STC = $0.001, 480 STC costs the user $0.48 for $24 worth of subscription. The cooldown only reduces how fast the damage occurs — it does not prevent undervaluation at low STC prices.

8.4 Optimal STC Amount at Launch

Given the platform will seed the AMM at approximately $0.01/STC (recommended launch), the 200 STC redemption represents an $2.00 cost for a $10 subscription — the user gets 80% off. While this is generous, it is acceptable if:

Arguments for keeping 200 STC

✓ Cooldown limits to ~2.4 uses/year per user

✓ Returned STC stays in treasury (not burned), recyclable

✓ Strong incentive for users to earn and hold STC

✓ Owner can raise the amount as price rises

✓ Creates powerful viral marketing hook

Arguments to raise above 200 STC

✗ At $0.001/STC, platform earns $0.20 per subscription

✗ Creators receive $0 from STC redemptions

✗ 200 STC takes only 40 days to earn (below the 100-day cooldown)

✗ Users can earn faster than they can redeem — treasury drain likely

✗ No USD floor — falls apart at low token prices

8.6 Recommended Structure

✅ Recommended: Phase-Based STC Subscription Amount

Phase 1 (Launch → STC < $0.02): 500 STC = 1 month Basic. At $0.01/STC this costs the user $5 — a 50% discount that is generous but not exploitable. Treasury receives $5 in STC value.

Phase 2 (STC $0.02–$0.05): Reduce to 300 STC. At $0.03/STC = $9 — near parity. Users feel rewarded as cost drops.

Phase 3 (STC > $0.05): Reduce to 200 STC. At $0.05/STC = $10 parity. The feature operates at full parity with fiat pricing.

All adjustments are made via updateSTCSubscriptionAmount() — no redeployment needed.

8.7 The STC-Treasury Recycling Advantage

Spent STC returns to platformTreasury (not burned). This is an important design choice that creates a recycling loop:

User earns STC (from userRewardsPool) → User spends 200 STC on subscription → 200 STC arrives at platformTreasury → Platform can: (a) re-seed AMM buyback reserve, (b) pay creator bonuses, (c) seed new liquidity, (d) burn for deflation → Circular economy: no new issuance required for this loop

This is architecturally superior to burning because it gives the platform flexibility. The treasury can decide dynamically whether to burn (deflationary) or recirculate (inflationary-neutral) based on market conditions.

Section 09

Strategic Recommendations

1
Add a Vesting Contract for the 300M Platform Development Allocation
Deploy an OpenZeppelin VestingWallet for the 15% platform allocation (300M STC). A 48-month linear vest with a 6-month cliff signals long-term commitment to investors and prevents a "team dump" perception. This is the single highest-impact trust signal a crypto expert will look for.
2
Add a Monthly Referral Cap to Prevent Pool Drain
The current referralReward has no per-user monthly cap. Add a mapping to track monthly referral claims and cap at 5 referrals/month per user (50 STC/month max from referrals). Without this, a botting attack could drain the rewards pool through fake referrals before the platform reaches scale.
3
Phase the STC Subscription Amount (500 → 300 → 200 STC)
Start at 500 STC at launch (protecting treasury during low-price phase), reduce to 300 STC when AMM price exceeds $0.02, then to 200 STC when price exceeds $0.05. Use updateSTCSubscriptionAmount() — no redeploy needed. This prevents treasury drain at launch while maintaining the feature's utility.
4
Implement Creator Staking Tiers (No Supply Increase Needed)
Add staking tiers for creators: stake 5k STC → 40% revenue share, 15k STC → 50%, 50k STC → 55%. This converts creators from net token sellers into net token lockers, extending the creator pool indefinitely and increasing aggregate staked supply — both bullish for STC price. 1,000 Pro Creators staking 15k STC each locks 15M STC.
5
Increase Buyback to 15% Once Platform Reaches $50k/mo Revenue
The current 8% buyback (hardcoded basis points in SepharSubscription.sol) is conservative. Once the platform reaches $50k/month, increasing to 15% buyback would turbocharge price appreciation. This requires redeployment with new basis-point constants but is the highest-leverage growth action available at scale.
6
Seed AMM with Minimum $10,000 USDC + 1,000,000 STC at Launch
This sets initial price at $0.01/STC. At this price, the 200 STC subscription costs $2 (users get 80% off), which is aggressive but the cooldown limits damage. With $10k seed, the AMM has enough depth to absorb 5,000 STC sells ($50) before noticeable slippage. Below this seed amount the AMM is too shallow for any meaningful trading.
7
Add addToStake() Function for Seamless Tier Upgrades
Users currently cannot add tokens to an existing stake position. This creates a UX cliff: a user who staked 1,000 STC for Tier 1 and later earns more cannot upgrade to Tier 2 without waiting for the full lock period to expire. An addToStake() function that adds tokens and recalculates the discount tier improves retention significantly.
8
Do NOT Increase Supply to 5B — Use Design to Extend Runway
Increasing supply dilutes the buyback price impact by 2.5×. Every $1 of buyback buys 2.5× fewer tokens, weakening the deflationary mechanism. Instead, use creator staking (locks supply) and dynamic reward caps (slows emission at scale) to extend runway. Scarcity is the primary long-term value driver.
Section 10

Founder Gain Strategy

As owner and co-founder, there are four distinct value-capture streams built into the contracts. Here is how to maximise each:

Stream 1 — Platform Operations Revenue (55% of all subscriptions)

Every USDC subscription flows 55% to platformTreasury. This is recurring, stable fiat-equivalent revenue. At 100,000 subscribers at $10/month: $550,000/month in USDC direct to the treasury you control. This is the primary income stream and the most important number to grow.

Stream 2 — AMM LP Fee Income (0.3% of every trade)

As the initial liquidity seeder, you receive a proportional share of every 0.3% swap fee. At $100,000/month in AMM volume: $300/month passive. As trading volume grows with adoption, this scales independently of subscriptions. Every user buying STC to stake or redeem subscriptions generates this fee.

Stream 3 — Token Appreciation on the 300M Platform Allocation

Stream 4 — Governance & Treasury Control

As onlyOwner you control: reward rates, STC subscription amount, buyback percentage, emergency pool rebalancing, and the ability to burn excess supply from the treasury. These levers allow you to actively manage token economics in response to market conditions — a significant structural advantage that no other token holder possesses.
