Section 5

Why Does the Token Price Grow?

Three forces work together to push STC price upward:

Force 1: Burning (Fewer Tokens Exist)

Every time STC is burned through the buyback, there are fewer tokens in existence. Imagine if every month someone shredded 80,000 limited-edition trading cards — the remaining cards become rarer and more valuable. Same principle here.

Force 2: Staking (Tokens Are Locked Away)

When users lock up STC for discounts, those tokens cannot be bought or sold until the lock period ends. If 50 million STC are locked by stakers, there are 50 million fewer tokens available on the market — pushing the price up naturally through reduced availability.

Force 3: The Revenue Multiplier

The platform's exchange has a built-in growth reward — as the platform earns more revenue, the displayed STC price gets a bonus multiplier applied. Think of it like a stock price premium tied to company performance:

It is a self-reinforcing cycle once the platform reaches critical mass.

Section 6

Should We Increase Supply to 5 Billion?

This question came up because more creators joining the platform means they need more STC as bonuses. The simple answer is: no — increasing supply would hurt more than help.

The Pizza Analogy

Imagine you own 1% of a pizza. Right now the pizza has 8 slices. You own 1 slice.

  • If we keep 2B: The pizza stays 8 slices. Your 1 slice keeps its value. As the pizza becomes more desirable (more people want it), your slice becomes more valuable.
  • If we go to 5B: Now the pizza is cut into 20 slices. You still own 1% — but that is 2 slices, each thinner. The pizza hasn't gotten bigger or better. It has just been cut into more pieces.

More importantly — every dollar the platform spends buying back and burning tokens has 2.5× less impact with 5B tokens than with 2B tokens. The buyback (the main price engine) becomes weaker.

The Real Solution: Make Creators Lock Up Tokens Instead of printing more tokens, we change the incentive for creators. Give them a reason to hold and lock STC rather than sell it. For example: lock 15,000 STC → earn 50% revenue share instead of 30%. If 1,000 creators do this, 15 million tokens are locked — solving the supply problem without printing a single new token.

Does the Cooldown Protect Us?

Yes — partially. The 100/200 day alternating cooldown means one user can only do this about 2.4 times per year maximum. So even if STC is very cheap, each user can only get 2.4 free months per year through this method. That limits the damage significantly, but does not fully protect at very low prices.

❌ The Gap Nobody Talks About When a user redeems STC for a subscription, the creators whose content they watch receive zero USDC from that transaction. The creator revenue split (30%), the buyback (8%), and the reward pool top-up (4%) only happen on fiat payments — not STC redemptions. The platform treasury gets the 200 STC, and that's it. Creators should know this.
